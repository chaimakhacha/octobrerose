Breast Cancer Awareness

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a complex disease that occurs when abnormal cells in the breast grow uncontrollably. These cells can form tumors that may spread to other parts of the body if left untreated.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

  • Lump or thickening in the breast or underarm
  • Changes in the shape or size of the breast
  • Dimpling of the skin
  • Nipple changes, such as inversion, scaling, or discharge
  • Redness or pitting of the skin over the breast
  • Pain in the breast or underarm

Risk Factors

  • Age: The risk generally increases with age.
  • Family history:Having a family history of breast cancer, particularly if you have certain gene mutations like BRCA1 or BRCA2, can elevate your risk.
  • Hormonal factors: Exposure to estrogen over a long period, such as starting menstruation early or going through menopause late, can increase the risk.
  • Lifestyle factors:Factors like obesity, physical inactivity, and alcohol consumption can contribute to an increased risk.
  • Medical History:Certain medical conditions, such as dense breast tissue or a personal history of certain benign breast conditions, may slightly increase the risk.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Diagnosis of breast cancer involves various tests, such as:

  • Mammography: An X-ray of the breast.
  • Ultrasound: Uses sound waves to create images of the breast.
  • Biopsy: A procedure to remove a small sample of tissue for examination under a microscope.

Treatment options depend on the stage of the cancer, its type, and your overall health. Common treatments include:

  • Surgery: To remove the tumor and sometimes nearby lymph nodes.
  • Chemotherapy: To kill cancer cells throughout the body.
  • Radiation therapy: To kill cancer cells in a specific area.
  • Hormonal therapy: To block hormones that fuel cancer growth.
  • Targeted therapy: To target specific molecules involved in cancer cell growth.

Prevention and Early Detection

While there's no guaranteed way to prevent breast cancer, adopting a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and maintaining a healthy weight, can reduce your risk. Early detection through regular self-exams and mammograms is crucial for better outcomes.
